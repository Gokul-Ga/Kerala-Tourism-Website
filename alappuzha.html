ALAPPUZHA


Alappuzha, also known as Alleppey, is a beautiful district located in the southern Indian state of Kerala. The district is known for its network of canals, lagoons, and waterways, earning it the nickname of "Venice of the East". Alappuzha is a popular tourist destination, offering visitors a range of experiences from backwater cruises and houseboat stays to exploring historic sites and experiencing the vibrant culture of Kerala.


Here are some of the top tourist places to visit in Alappuzha:




Vembanad Lake

Esteemed as the ‘Backwater Capital of India’ or the ‘Venice of the East’, Alleppey is known for its silent backwaters and bountiful beauty!

Alappuzha Beach

This beach along with its famous backwater houseboats and boat races attracts thousands of tourists every year. The beach is is one of the most popular picnic spots in Alappuzha. Earlier known an Alleppey, the place is now famous for the Snake Boat Races held here every year around the time of the Onam festival.

Kuttanad

Kuttanad is historically important in the ancient history of South India and is the major rice producer in the state. Farmers of Kuttanad are famous for Biosaline Farming.

Pathiramanal Island

The exquisite island is home to around 50 species of migratory birds and 91 species of local birds. A must visit spot for avid bird watchers.

Krishnapuram Palace

It is a historically important palace, located close to the Sree Krishnaswamy Temple at Krishnapuram. History reckons this double-storeyed palace as the one where the kings of Kayamkulam dynasty last stayed. It is a typical example of a palace design that is akin to that of local rulers in ancient Kerala.
